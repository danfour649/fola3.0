# Structure Decision Guide: Sole Proprietorship vs Spouse Partnership

Date: 2026-05-02  
Region: New Brunswick, Canada  
For: Fola's Cleaning Company planning

---

## 1) Purpose

Use this worksheet to choose between:

- **Option A:** Sole proprietorship (one owner)
- **Option B:** General partnership with spouse (two owners)

This is a practical planning tool, not legal or tax advice.

---

## 2) Fast comparison

### Sole proprietorship (one owner)

**Pros**

- Simplest startup and administration
- One-owner decision speed
- Straightforward tax reporting flow

**Cons**

- No liability shield
- No true co-ownership for spouse
- Harder to formalize shared equity without restructuring later

### Spouse general partnership (two owners)

**Pros**

- Clear co-ownership and role sharing
- Profit/loss allocation between partners
- Better fit when both spouses actively run the business

**Cons**

- No liability shield
- More setup and recordkeeping complexity
- Partnership agreement strongly recommended
- Additional tax filing complexity may apply in some cases

## 4) Tie-breaker questions

Answer yes/no.

1. Will both spouses work consistently in the business every week?
2. Will both spouses make management decisions and accept operational responsibility?
3. Do you want legal co-ownership from day one (not just payroll support)?
4. Are you comfortable documenting and maintaining a partnership agreement?
5. Are you ready for increased accounting/tax administration complexity?

Guidance:

- **Mostly "No"** -> Sole proprietorship is often the cleaner start.
- **Mostly "Yes"** -> Spouse partnership may better match reality.

---

## 5) Red-flag checklist before final decision

If any item below is unresolved, pause registration and resolve first:

- [ ] No written role definition for each spouse
- [ ] No agreement on who can sign contracts and spend money
- [ ] No agreement on profit withdrawals
- [ ] No agreement on what happens if one spouse stops working in business
- [ ] No agreement on what happens in illness/separation/death scenarios
- [ ] No insurance plan aligned to structure
- [ ] No bookkeeping ownership and controls assigned

---

## 6) Spouse partnership minimum agreement terms (recommended)

If choosing a partnership, document at least:

- Ownership percentages
- Capital contributions (cash/tools/vehicle/time)
- Profit and loss allocation formula
- Decision rights (day-to-day vs major decisions)
- Banking authority and spending limits
- Partner compensation/draw rules
- Admission/removal/retirement rules
- Dispute process and buyout formula
- Dissolution and wind-down process

---

## 7) Tax and compliance reminders (Canada/NB)

- Both sole proprietorship and partnership income generally flow to personal returns.
- Partnerships may have additional information-return obligations in certain situations.
- If one spouse is self-employed, filing deadlines can affect both spouses' return timing.
- Balance owing is still generally due by the payment deadline to avoid interest.
- Keep records for role, compensation, and allocation logic.
