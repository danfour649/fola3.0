# Step-by-Step Launch Guide (New Brunswick, Canada)

Use this sequence to open and run a legitimate cleaning company in New Brunswick.

## 1) Define your service model

- Choose your core services:
  - Residential recurring cleaning
  - Move-in/move-out cleaning
  - Commercial janitorial cleaning
  - Specialized services (post-construction, carpet, windows, etc.)
- Decide where you will operate first (city/town and surrounding radius).
- Decide whether you will start solo or with employees/subcontractors.

## 2) Choose a legal structure

Common options:

- **Sole proprietorship**: simplest setup, but no legal separation from you.
- **Corporation**: separate legal entity, more administration, better liability separation.
- **Partnership**: if you have co-owners.

For structure choice, confirm with an accountant/lawyer based on liability and tax planning.

## 3) Choose and register your business name

- Search and reserve a name where required.
- Register through Service New Brunswick (Corporate Registry / BRS Online).
- If operating as a sole proprietor only under your exact legal name, different rules may apply; verify before filing.

## 4) Register with CRA (BN and tax accounts)

Use CRA **Business Registration Online (BRO)** to set up:

- Business Number (BN)
- GST/HST account (RT), if required or chosen
- Payroll deductions account (RP), if hiring

For most businesses, GST/HST registration becomes mandatory once taxable revenue exceeds the CRA small-supplier threshold.

## 5) Confirm permits and municipal requirements

- Run your activity through **NB BizPaL** (federal/provincial/municipal permits).
- Contact your municipality directly (zoning/home occupation/signage/business licence, if applicable).
- Keep copies of all approvals in this repo.

## 6) Set up business insurance and risk controls

At minimum, price and arrange:

- Commercial general liability (CGL)
- Commercial auto (if business vehicles)
- Property/equipment coverage
- Bonding (often requested by commercial clients)

Set up basic risk controls: PPE, WHMIS process, incident reporting, and site-specific hazard checks.

## 7) Register with WorkSafeNB when required

- Employers with mandatory coverage thresholds must register with WorkSafeNB within the required period.
- If you do not meet mandatory thresholds, review voluntary/personal coverage options.

## 8) Build employment compliance before hiring

- Set wage, overtime, scheduling, and payroll to comply with NB Employment Standards.
- Use written job offers and clear role expectations.
- Create onboarding and training records.

## 9) Build your legal/operational document set

Before first client:

- Client quote template
- Service agreement template
- Scope-of-work checklist
- Invoice template and payment terms
- Incident report form

Store all finalized versions in this repository.

## 10) Set up finance operations

- Open a dedicated business bank account.
- Choose bookkeeping software/process.
- Track HST collected and paid (if registered).
- Separate owner draws/payroll from operating expenses.

## 11) Launch with controlled capacity

- Start with manageable routes and service windows.
- Track actual job time and margin per job.
- Tighten scope language to prevent unpaid extras.

## 12) Run ongoing compliance cycles

Create recurring reminders for:

- Annual return/renewals (where applicable)
- Insurance renewals
- GST/HST filings (if registered)
- Payroll remittances and slips (if hiring)
- WorkSafeNB reporting and assessments (if applicable)

---

## Quick startup order (practical)

1. Service model + pricing
2. Structure decision
3. Name registration (SNB)
4. CRA BN / tax accounts
5. BizPaL + municipal confirmations
6. Insurance
7. WorkSafeNB review/registration
8. Contracts + templates
9. Client acquisition + launch
